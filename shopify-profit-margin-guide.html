What Is Shopify Profit Margin?

Shopify profit margin is the percentage of revenue that remains after deducting product cost, shipping, payment processing fees, platform fees, and advertising expenses.

The basic formula is:

Profit Margin (%) = (Net Profit ÷ Revenue) × 100

What Is Considered a Good Profit Margin?

  • Low margin: 5%–15%
  • Healthy margin: 20%–30%
  • Excellent margin: 30%–50%

For dropshipping stores running paid ads, most profitable brands target at least 25% net margin after advertising.

Example Calculation

Selling price: $39
Product cost: $10
Shipping: $5
Transaction fee (3%): $1.17
Ad cost per sale: $15

Net profit = $39 - 10 - 5 - 1.17 - 15 = $7.83

Profit margin = 7.83 ÷ 39 = 20%

Why Many Shopify Stores Fail

Most stores fail because they underestimate:

  • Advertising cost volatility
  • Refund rates
  • Payment processing fees
  • Discount impact

Without understanding margin, scaling ads becomes risky.

How to Increase Your Shopify Profit Margin

1. Raise Average Order Value (AOV)

Bundle products, add upsells, and use post-purchase offers.

2. Negotiate Supplier Pricing

Even reducing product cost by $1 can dramatically increase margin percentage.

3. Improve Ad Targeting

Lower cost per acquisition directly increases net profit.

4. Reduce Refund Rate

Clear product descriptions and better customer support reduce hidden losses.

Final Thoughts

A good Shopify profit margin is not about chasing high revenue. It is about building a business that remains profitable even when advertising costs rise.

Always calculate your true net profit before scaling.
