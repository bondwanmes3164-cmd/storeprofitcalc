Break Even ROAS: What It Is and How to Calculate It

If you're running paid ads for Shopify or dropshipping, understanding your break even ROAS is critical. Without it, you don't know whether your ads are actually profitable.

What Is Break Even ROAS?

Break even ROAS (Return on Ad Spend) is the minimum ROAS required to avoid losing money. It tells you the exact point where revenue equals total cost.

If your actual ROAS is below break even, you are losing money. If it is above break even, you are profitable.

Break Even ROAS Formula

Break Even ROAS = 1 ÷ Net Profit Margin

Where net profit margin is expressed as a decimal.

Example

If your net margin is 25% (0.25):

Break Even ROAS = 1 ÷ 0.25 = 4.0

This means you must generate $4 in revenue for every $1 spent on ads just to break even.

How to Calculate Net Profit Margin First

To find your break even ROAS, you must first calculate your real net margin:

Net Profit = Selling Price − Product Cost − Shipping − Transaction Fees − Advertising Cost

Why Break Even ROAS Is Important

  • Prevents scaling unprofitable campaigns
  • Helps set realistic ROAS targets
  • Protects cash flow
  • Improves ad optimization decisions

Many store owners focus only on high ROAS numbers without understanding their cost structure. A ROAS of 3 might look good — but if your break even ROAS is 3.5, you're losing money.

How to Improve Your Break Even ROAS

Increase Profit Margin

Higher margin lowers required break even ROAS.

Reduce Product Cost

Negotiating better supplier pricing improves margin.

Increase Average Order Value (AOV)

Bundles and upsells reduce the ROAS threshold.

Final Thoughts

Break even ROAS is not just a metric — it is your advertising safety line. Before scaling, always calculate this number.
