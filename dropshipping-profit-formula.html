Dropshipping Profit Formula: How to Calculate Real Net Profit

The Basic Dropshipping Profit Formula

Net Profit = Selling Price − Product Cost − Shipping − Transaction Fees − Advertising Cost

Profit Margin (%) = (Net Profit ÷ Selling Price) × 100

Cost Breakdown Explained

1. Product Cost

The amount you pay your supplier for the product.

2. Shipping Cost

Either paid to supplier or absorbed into product cost.

3. Transaction Fees

Usually 2%–3% from payment processors such as Stripe or PayPal.

4. Advertising Cost

Your cost per acquisition (CPA) from Facebook Ads, TikTok Ads, or Google Ads. This is often the largest expense.

Example Scenario

Selling price: $49
Product cost: $18
Shipping: $6
Transaction fee (3%): $1.47
Ad cost per sale: $20

Net Profit = 49 - 18 - 6 - 1.47 - 20 = $3.53

Margin = 3.53 ÷ 49 = 7.2%

This example shows why many dropshipping stores struggle — advertising eats profit.

What Is a Good Dropshipping Margin?

  • Under 10% → Hard to scale
  • 15–25% → Sustainable
  • 30%+ → Strong scaling potential

How to Improve Profit

Increase Selling Price Strategically

Improve branding and perceived value to justify higher pricing.

Lower CPA

Test better creatives and optimize targeting.

Upsell and Bundle

Increase average order value without increasing ad spend.

Final Advice

Never scale based on revenue alone. Always calculate your net margin before increasing ad budget.
