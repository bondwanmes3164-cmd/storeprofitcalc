How to Price Dropshipping Products in 2026

Pricing is one of the biggest reasons dropshipping stores fail. Set it too low and you lose money. Set it too high and you kill conversion rate.

If you want to test different pricing scenarios instantly, use our profit calculator.

Step 1: Know Your Total Cost

Before choosing a selling price, you must calculate your full cost per order:

Total Cost = Product Cost + Shipping + Transaction Fees + Advertising Cost

If you don’t include advertising cost, your pricing will be dangerously inaccurate.

Step 2: Decide Your Target Profit Margin

Most profitable dropshipping stores aim for:

  • 20%–30% net margin (sustainable)
  • 30%+ for aggressive scaling

If you're unsure what margin is healthy, read our Shopify Profit Margin Guide.

Step 3: Calculate Required Selling Price

Selling Price = Total Cost ÷ (1 − Target Margin)

Example

If total cost is $25 and you want 25% margin:

Selling Price = 25 ÷ (1 − 0.25) = 25 ÷ 0.75 = $33.33

This ensures your pricing supports your profitability goals.

Common Pricing Strategies

2x or 3x Markup Method

Simple but risky. It ignores advertising cost and can destroy margin.

Psychological Pricing

Using prices like $29.99 instead of $30 to improve perceived value.

Value-Based Pricing

Charge based on perceived benefit rather than cost alone. Branding and positioning become critical here.

Why Many Dropshipping Stores Underprice

  • Ignoring CPA (cost per acquisition)
  • Copying competitor prices blindly
  • Focusing only on conversion rate
  • Failing to calculate break even ROAS

If you don’t know your advertising safety line, read our Break Even ROAS Guide.

Final Thoughts

Pricing is not about guessing. It is about building margin that protects you from rising ad costs and refunds.

Before launching a product, always calculate your real numbers.
